Jengappa Blast

Signature Drink

Flavor Profile

Citrus-melon base like Baja Blast → layered with kokum tang, a touch of cardamom mist, and a chill-kick from mint + green chili essence. Tropical, spicy, and bright.
Taste: bright citrus → tangy kokum → sweet-melon → whisper of spice.
NYC energy + Bengaluru soul.

Ingredients

Single 12–14 oz serving
  • ½ cup lime juice (fresh)
  • ¼ cup nimbu soda or club soda
  • ¾ cup cold melon soda (honeydew or Mountain Dew as fallback)
  • 2 tbsp kokum syrup
  • 1 tsp jaggery syrup (or brown sugar syrup)
  • 2–3 mint leaves, slapped for aroma
  • 1 drop green chili tincture (optional but makes it pop)
  • Tiny pinch cardamom
  • Ice—crushed

Method

  1. Fill a chilled glass with crushed ice.
  2. Shake together: lime juice + kokum syrup + jaggery syrup + a pinch of cardamom.
  3. Pour over ice.
  4. Add melon soda + nimbu soda for fizz.
  5. Toss in mint.
  6. Finish with one drop of green-chili tincture for a warm after-kick.

Alternate Versions

1. Jengappa Blast Zero (Sugar-Free)
Swap kokum syrup for kokum water extract. Use diet lemon-lime soda + a few drops of stevia. Add a spritz of ginger cold-press for bite.
2. Jengappa Blast Creamstorm
Add 1 tbsp sweetened condensed milk. Shake vigorously. Tastes like Baja Blast meets Bengaluru falooda vibes.
3. Jengappa Street Heat
Rim the glass with chili-salt + chaat masala. Add ½ tsp raw mango (amchur) syrup. Massive flavor punch.
4. Jengappa Night Mode (Cocktail)
1 oz vodka or white rum. ½ oz kokum liqueur (if available). Everything else the same. Perfect high-energy summer drink.

Presentation

Optional but killer
Serve in tall glass. Teal + ruby swirl from kokum gives that Baja Blast visual twist. Mint crown on top.
